## 3. Emerging EdTech Trends (2025-2026)

### 3.1 AI Is Now Table Stakes
From the research, AI has moved from "nice to have" to **expected baseline** in school software:
- **97% of district leaders** see AI potential (SETDA 2025)
- **AI surpasses cybersecurity** as #1 state edtech priority for the first time (SETDA 2025)
- **70% of US schools** projected to adopt AI for at least one core administrative function
- Indian ERPs (Schoolites, ShikshaSetu, Edoovi, RexoCampus, TACHY) all now have AI features as core differentiators

**Where the market is going:**
- AI agents that handle repetitive tasks autonomously (admission queries, fee reminders, substitute teacher suggestions)
- "Chat with your data" — natural language querying of school databases
- AI-generated content (lesson plans, assessments, report cards, feedback notes)
- Predictive analytics (at-risk student prediction, fee defaulter prediction, academic outcome prediction)
- AI tutoring integrated with school data (not standalone)

### 3.2 Student Wellness & Mental Health
A major emerging category:
- **Alongside app** — 24/7 AI chatbot for student mental health, mood tracking, journaling, psychoeducational resources, 120+ AI chat modules, 30+ languages
- **MEandMine** — gamified emotional regulation, daily emotion check-ins, 200+ personalized games, AI analyzes 160+ in-game behaviors to identify struggling students
- **WellMind** — screen time control, emotional state tracking, mindfulness exercises, professional support connection
- **Elggo** — bilingual mental health platform for schools, 25+ lessons, 500+ resources, real-time wellbeing analytics, clinical safeguarding with flagging

**No Indian school ERP has wellness features.** This is a wide-open gap.

### 3.3 Mobile-First Parent Engagement
- **95% of parents own smartphones** — mobile-first is now standard
- **39% prefer mobile app** for school updates
- **70% favor texts/push** for urgent alerts
- Parents want: convenience, personalization, timeliness
- Districts with increased digital communication see **higher parent satisfaction and engagement**

### 3.4 Data Privacy & Compliance
- **India's DPDP Act 2023** is now a compliance requirement, not optional
- Schools need: consent management, data export/portability, right to erasure, audit trails, data residency in India
- **NEP 2020 compliance** — holistic progress cards, competency-based assessments, 5+3+3+4 structure, UDISE+ reporting
- **APAAR ID** — new government mandate for student identity
- Multiple Indian ERPs now advertise DPDP compliance and NEP alignment as core features

### 3.5 Facial Recognition & Biometric Attendance
- Multiple Indian ERPs now offer face recognition attendance (Edoovi, TACHY, Schoolites)
- Face login for student apps (TACHY) — solves the "young students can't remember passwords" problem
- Visitor face recognition for school security (Edoovi)
- QR code + RFID + Face — multi-modal attendance (EduScan)
- Consent-based, DPDP-compliant (liveness detection, optional enrollment)

### 3.6 WhatsApp as Primary Channel
- Schoolites has official WhatsApp Business API with AI auto-reply
- TACHY offers WhatsApp as alternative to app for all notifications
- Class ON App uses WhatsApp for notifications
- **WhatsApp is the #1 communication channel for Indian parents** — apps that don't leverage it are at a disadvantage

## 6. The Brutal Truth

```
Vidya Prayag today is a well-built app with:
  ✅ Solid fundamentals (attendance, marks, homework, messaging, analytics)
  ✅ Good architecture (KMP, Compose, Ktor, Exposed)
  ✅ Nice UX (V Design System, portal navigation)
  ✅ School discovery (unique, but incomplete)
  ✅ OTP infrastructure (6 providers, gateway device)

But is missing vs. the market:
  ❌ Online fee payment (every competitor has this)
  ❌ NEP 2020 / UDISE+ compliance (government mandate)
  ❌ DPDP Act compliance (legal requirement)
  ❌ WhatsApp for general notifications (not just OTP)
  ❌ Transport tracking (standard in Indian ERPs)
  ❌ Library, hostel, payroll, inventory (standard modules)
  ❌ AI features (competitors are AI-first now)
  ❌ Board-specific report cards (every Indian ERP has this)
  ❌ Multi-branch support
  ❌ Student wellness (emerging but critical)
  ❌ Student app/portal
  ❌ Live classes / LMS
```

**The good news:** The architecture is sound, the codebase is clean, and the unique features (discovery, gamification, family circle, passport) are genuinely differentiated. The gap is in **breadth of standard ERP modules** and **AI integration** — both are catch-up work, not innovation risk.

**The strategy:** Don't try to build 50 modules. Build the **Phase 0 survival features** first (fee payment, compliance, WhatsApp), then **leapfrog with blue ocean features** (parent gamification, family circle, AI tutor, wellness) that no competitor has. Win on **depth + differentiation**, not on module count.
